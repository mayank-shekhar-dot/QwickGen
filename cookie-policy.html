01 What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer, smartphone, tablet, or other device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work, improve their performance, and provide information to website owners.

Think of cookies as small memory files for websites. They allow a website to "remember" your actions and preferences over time, so you don't have to re-enter information every time you visit the site or navigate between pages.

How Cookies Work

When you visit QuickGenAI, our web server may send a cookie to your browser, which stores it on your device. The next time you visit, your browser sends the cookie back to the server. This allows the website to recognize your browser and remember things like your preferences.

Cookie Duration

  • Session Cookies: These are temporary cookies that expire when you close your browser. They are used to maintain your session during a single visit.
  • Persistent Cookies: These cookies remain on your device for a set period or until you manually delete them. They are used to remember your preferences across multiple visits.

🔑 Cookies themselves do not contain personally identifiable information. They typically contain a unique identifier associated with an anonymous user profile that helps websites recognize returning visitors.

04 Essential Cookies

Essential cookies (also called "strictly necessary" cookies) are fundamental to the operation of QuickGenAI. Without these cookies, the website cannot function properly.

What Essential Cookies Do

  • Enable navigation between pages without losing context
  • Maintain the functionality of our AI tools during your session
  • Support form submissions and prevent duplicate submissions
  • Enable security features and fraud detection
  • Remember your cookie consent preference
  • Support technical load balancing across our servers

Can I Disable Essential Cookies?

Essential cookies cannot be disabled through cookie consent settings because they are fundamental to how the website works. If you disable them via browser settings, parts of QuickGenAI may not function correctly. They do not collect personal information for marketing or profiling purposes.

✅ Essential cookies are always active and do not require your consent under most privacy regulations, as they are strictly necessary for service delivery.

05 Analytics Cookies

Analytics cookies help us understand how visitors interact with QuickGenAI. This understanding allows us to improve the platform, fix issues, and provide a better experience for all users.

Google Analytics

We use Google Analytics, a web analytics service provided by Google LLC. Google Analytics uses cookies to collect information about how visitors use our website. This information is used to compile reports and to help us improve our Platform.

The information collected by Google Analytics cookies includes:

  • Pages visited and time spent on each page
  • How you arrived at QuickGenAI (e.g., search engine, direct link)
  • Device type, browser type, and operating system
  • Geographic region (country/city level, not precise location)
  • User flow through the website (navigation patterns)

Data Anonymization

Google Analytics data is aggregated and anonymized. We have enabled IP anonymization, which means your full IP address is never stored by Google Analytics. Analytics data is not used to identify individual users.

Opting Out of Analytics

You can opt out of Google Analytics tracking by installing the Google Analytics Opt-Out Browser Add-on. This add-on prevents Google Analytics from collecting information about your visits.

06 Advertising Cookies

QuickGenAI is a free platform, and advertising helps us sustain our services. Advertising cookies are used to deliver relevant advertisements to you on and off our platform.

How Advertising Cookies Work

Advertising cookies work by building a profile of your interests based on websites you visit. This profile is then used to show you advertisements that are more likely to be relevant to your interests. The process is largely automated by advertising platforms like Google AdSense.

Advertising cookies may:

  • Remember that you have visited our website
  • Help deliver personalized ads based on your browsing interests
  • Limit how many times you see the same ad
  • Measure the effectiveness of advertising campaigns
  • Identify whether you came to our site after seeing an ad

Opting Out of Personalized Advertising

You can opt out of personalized advertising by visiting Google's Ad Settings. You can also visit the Your Online Choices website or Digital Advertising Alliance to opt out of interest-based advertising. Note that opting out does not prevent you from seeing ads — it means ads may be less relevant to you.

07 Third-Party Cookies

In addition to our own cookies, QuickGenAI works with various third-party services that may set their own cookies on your device. These third-party cookies are set by external companies and are subject to those companies' own privacy and cookie policies.

Categories of Third-Party Cookie Providers

  • Advertising Networks: Google AdSense and its advertising partners
  • Analytics Providers: Google Analytics and similar services
  • AI Service Providers: APIs that power our AI tools may set session-level cookies
  • Content Delivery Networks (CDNs): Services that deliver website assets efficiently
  • Social Media Plugins: If social sharing buttons are present, those platforms may set cookies

Your Control Over Third-Party Cookies

You have the right to accept or refuse third-party cookies. You can do this through your browser settings (see the Managing Cookies section below) or through the opt-out mechanisms provided by specific third parties.

For a comprehensive list of third-party cookie providers and opt-out options, visit Your Online Choices.

09 Managing & Disabling Cookies

You have full control over cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to:

  • View all cookies stored on your device
  • Delete some or all cookies
  • Block cookies from specific websites
  • Block all third-party cookies
  • Set your browser to notify you when cookies are set
  • Browse in private/incognito mode (which doesn't save cookies after the session)
