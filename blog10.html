AI for Saving Dying Languages by Simulating Their Future Evolution

Languages don't go extinct, they morph into something else until they can no longer be formed (via cultural assimilation, etc.).

Many languages are effectively "dead" due to their communities evolving towards a majority language (i.e. the dominant modern languages: English, Mandarin, Hindi and Spanish). AI has the potential to assist in the preservation of these endangered languages by modelling their future evolution using linguistic and anthropological knowledge combined with machine learning technology.

AI modelling of language evolution can help to preserve endangered languages not only by documenting and transcribing spoken language or by creating an online database to document the languages' evolution, but also to make predictions about how these languages will evolve. For example;

  • How the grammar will change
  • Which words will be created (neologisms)
  • How will we use technology to continue to grow language
  • How we will survive in a "digital" world

Until now these concepts have been known in various disciplines (such as linguistics, anthropology and machine learning) and have existed side by side. However, the concept of AI language evolution modelling is emerging and will be a new discipline encompassing all three fields.

The Problem To Solve

The problem with traditional methods of preserving endangered languages is:

  • Recording Native Speakers
  • Writing Grammatical Rules
  • Creating a Dictionary

The above methods have several limitations:

  • ❌ The methods "freeze" the languages in time, meaning they cease to be alive when the documentation is complete. Therefore language growth would cease once it is documented.
  • ❌ The methods rely upon fluent speakers, and many endangered languages are only spoken by a handful of speakers.
  • ❌ The methods do not appeal to younger generations. Unless a language can adapt to contemporary sounds and ideas it will no longer be of use to younger generations.
  • ❌ The methods do not include any relationship or integration into digital ecology. Without an online presence/model, the language will quickly disappear from the world.

AI provides us with a new approach to preserving endangered languages and revitalising them.

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is capable of simulating the future evolution of a given culture's language through modelling. Similar to its existing capabilities:

  • Climate Predictions
  • DNA Mutation Predictions
  • Market Behaviour
  • Social Trends

We know that language exists as a system of systems. The evolution of the language system into its final form results from the evolution of several factors.

Artificial Intelligence (AI) has the ability to learn about:

  • Phonological rules (what sounds mean)
  • Grammar (the way a language is put together)
  • Vocabulary (commonly used words)
  • How metaphors are used in a culture
  • How the environment impacts the vocabulary of a culture

Using this information, AI can predict how the language will continue to evolve under various scenarios.

Predictions on Evolution of Language by Scientific Means

Both to replicate & predict a language's evolution, Artificial Intelligence (AI) will only be effective when it has several important elements:

1. Historical Linguistic Analysis

To predict how languages evolve, AI will be based on how similar languages in the past evolved.

For instance, If we could see how Proto-Indo-European (PIE), Sanskrit, Hindi changed through time due to phonetic shifts, then AI could do the same thing for endangered languages.

2. Reconstructive Neural Language Modeling

AI can replace missing vocabulary in a language.

Examples of what it could replace include:

  • Missing nouns
  • Phrasal verbs which may have disappeared
  • Missing phonological rules
  • Predicted changes
  • Meaning Changes

This is similar to how AI attempts to replicate digital images that have been altered.

3. Simulation of Cultural Environments

Culture defines how a language will evolve.

AI will therefore simulate our cultural aspects such as:

  • Traditions
  • Geography
  • Occupations
  • Migrations
  • Technological Environment

For example, if Indigenous Inuit language or Culture encounters the Digital Environment, AI will anticipate the evolution of New Words to describe Digital Products such as Apps, Websites, etc.

4. Simulated Social Behavior

It can also predict:

  • How slang is formed
  • The spread of Newly Formed Words
  • The merging of Dialects
  • The changes of Bilingual Communities

As such, They will create a Dynamic Representation of Endangered Languages.

The Future of AI Language Ownership: Ethical Perspectives

There are some major questions that arise from this area of research.

  • ⚠️ Who has ownership of the AI generated language? The communities have ownership of the language, not the corporations.
  • ⚠️ Is it good to give AI the right to create new words for cultures? AI should have the right to create new words for cultures, but only with input and consent from the traditional community.
  • ⚠️ Is it possible that AI would alter the true meaning of the language? Protective measures will be necessary to prevent any misrepresentation of a culture through AI.
  • ⚠️ Should children in the future learn this language? Only if the community enhances the language's development and evolution, and the community would support that.
  • ⚠️ Is an AI simulation of the language authentic? Languages have always changed; therefore, authenticity is not static.

The empowerment to grow a language is the goal of AI language usage -- not to replace anything.

A future language would not be an object locked in a museum, but instead, continually growing.
