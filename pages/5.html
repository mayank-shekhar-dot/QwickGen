Limitations of AI in Creativity: Where Machines Fail

AI May Help but Is Limited

AI can produce ideas, sort patterns, and make suggestions—but it does so quickly, and there are limits to its true creativity. It can adopt styles, build off ideas that exist, and make suggestions---but it is not capable of creating something new from imagination or emotion.

Why Humans Are Still In Demand

Humans include emotions, unique experiences, and intuitiveness as part of a creative process. For example, a painter depicts art that is sprung from some memory or feeling he's had. A writer completes a novel based on personal experience. AI could not manifest those feelings, emotions or create art from those experiences. AI only analyzes existing data without feeling.

The Limits of AI

AI cannot feel joy or sadness, be inspired, or feel as humans do. AI cannot understand humor, irony, or deep meaning, like a human can. While humans can observe the world around them and make connections from unique human experiences, AI can simply develop patterns of observation and present them. AI is also not able to qualify subjective decisions associated with meaning or determine which decisions may be more complicated.

How Humans and AI Can Work Together

Even with limits, AI can still provide support for humans. AI can generate drafts, and suggestions, and save time. Humans can take those drafts or suggestions and create or build on intuitive creativity, emotion, and originality. The culmination of AI and human creativity allows both to flourish, instead of rendering one in need of the other.
