Utilizing AI for Learning Rather Than Cheating

Understanding AI in learning

AI is rapidly emerging as a part of education. It can facilitate faster learning, improved understanding, and valuable time management. For students, there are even tools like quickgenai that will explain topics, summarize lessons, or write notes. While AI tools and learning is a benefit for students, we must ensure we are using AI as an advantage, rather than a shortcut for cheating.

How students can use AI properly

You can use AI tools to understand topics or concepts, not to copy answers. If you're stuck on a question, you may want to ask the AI for an explanation of the topic. However, make sure you first give the problem your full focus. Once you're finished, you can ask the AI to explain the problem again or check to ensure your answer is correct. AI is meant to be your assistant, not your replacement.

Examples of using AI for learning

AI is a good way to summarizing chapters in a book when you don't have time to read the entire chapter. Use AI as the grammar checker for your essay. You can use AI to learn new vocabulary. You can also use the AI to break down coding mistakes or science experiments when you don't understand the concepts. However, do not submit an AI-generated answer as your answer.

Why cheating with AI isn't beneficial

While cheating with AI may seem like a short-cut, it's actively sabotaging your learning process. If you plan on relying on AI fully, you are going to miss out on building the necessary skills and concept that are required for future learning.
