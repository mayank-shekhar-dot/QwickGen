Overview: You Leave Your Digital Fingerprint Without Even Knowing It

Every time you type out a message - whether it be quickly or slowly, formally or chaotically, full of emojis or perfectly punctuated - you are not only using words to communicate with someone else.

You are creating a "digital signature", otherwise known as an AI Fingerprint.

Researchers have named this AI Fingerprint an AI Fingerprint because of its different ways to communicate and the patterns associated with them, an example of which is your AI Fingerprint can help an AI System to identify how you communicate, but does not tell it what you are communicating.

Your AI Fingerprint is not connected to any of your Personal Information (e.g. Name, Address), however it can be identified by your Digital Behaviour, i.e. the choices you naturally make when you type, speak or interact with other People Online.

What is an AI Fingerprint?

The AI Fingerprint is the collection of the behaviours that make your Digital Communication Unique to You.

Your AI Fingerprint is not a 'real' fingerprint, nor is it a biological fingerprint; instead, it is a 'style-based' fingerprint:

Your Style:

  • The way you use your Grammar.
  • The rhythms you use in your Sentences.
  • The Words you Choose to Use.
  • The Mistakes you Typically Make.
  • The Use of Emojis.
  • The Speed at which you Write.
  • The Tone in Which you Communicate.
  • The Pacing of your Digital Communication.

These Characteristics can be combined together to Create Something that is not exactly the Same as Anyone Else's. It is something that can be Recognised by AI, and Think of it as your "Digital Vibe".

The Benefits of AI Fingerprinting (The Bright Side)

The primary function of AIfingerprints is to provide a user a better experience.

1. Create Personalization:

A person's style of writing can be used by application developers to improve their applications' ability to provide more optimal suggestions, recommendations, and learning preferences based upon how they communicate (the way they write).

2. Identifying Bots:

Fake identities do not have the ability to copy or emulate human behaviour in a consistent manner. An understanding of your AIfingerprint will aid companies in confirming your authenticity without having to expose your identity.

3. Developing Writing Tools:

Smart applications are programmed to use your preferences to produce an output that represents you more accurately.

4. Protecting Accounts:

When an account is disrupted by someone else who uses different styles and idioms, AIfingerprints can be used by the network system to identify that the account was compromised (without disclosing the identity of the intruder).
