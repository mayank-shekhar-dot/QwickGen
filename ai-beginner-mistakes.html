AI Mistakes Beginners Make & How to Avoid Them

This is a practical manual for the novice user of AI; students, creators and others who may have little prior experience of learning about Artificial Intelligence.

1. Thinking that Artificial Intelligence is Similar to Google

- Many beginners think of Google-like services when they ask questions of AI, whereby they will tend to ask short, vague questions expecting that Artificial Intelligence will return with short, concise, near-perfect answers. There are some limitations in the use of this approach based upon how the AI responds to users based on it's own data patterns of relevance, not based on real-time factual data of the topic.

• Examples of this are that when asking an AI about the process of photosynthesis you may input a question like "explain photosynthesis" instead of asking the AI a clear and detailed description such as "explain photosynthesis in simple bullet points suitable for a 9th-grade level student".

2. Accepting AI Information as Being Accurate

• This occurs when a user accepts any information provided by Artificial Intelligence without verification prior to assuming or believing it to be correct.

• AI can provide outdated and incorrectly stated information to users.

• Cross-checking important, relevant factual-based data from different sources of credible information is necessary prior to believing anything is accurate relative to the requested topic. Users should always check textbooks or official website sources for confirmation.

Artificial Intelligence is utilised as a tool to support users, and should not be confused with being the ultimate or final source of factual information.

3. Using AI as a Means of Cheating Rather Than Learning

• Some users will utilise Artificial Intelligence to create homework assignments, essays, etc. that are 100% generated by the AI.

• This approach does not permit growth or skill set development in the user. The majority of educators will be able to detect this method of cheating by reviewing submitted assignments.

⭐ 4. Provide clear instructions to AI

Mistake:

An example of a vague prompt would be: “Improve this.”

Why it’s a problem:

There are many different interpretations of what it is to “improve” something, and AI does not inherently understand any single one of those.

How to fix:

  • ✔ Use clear language
  • ✔ Use a specific tone, style, word count, and purpose
  • ✔ Provide an example of a successful project

An example of a clear prompt is:

✔ “Rewrite this using plain English, 100-150 words, in a friendly tone.”

⭐ 5. Failing to ask for additional clarification

Mistake:

People often stop after receiving the first response from AI.

Why it’s a problem:

Because AI functions best when it is used as part of an extended conversation.

How to avoid:

  • ✔ Request clarifying statements
  • ✔ Request alternatives to the original response
  • ✔ Request a step-by-step explanation
  • ✔ Request additional examples or visual aids

AI gets smarter as more context is added to a conversation.

⭐ 6. Entering your identity information

Mistake:

Some new users may enter: “My Address is…”, “My School is…”, “My Phone Number is…”, etc.

Why it’s a problem:

Entering this type of information can create a security risk as well as it’s not needed.

How to avoid:

  • ✔ Never enter any form of your identity
  • ✔ Enter general information instead
  • ✔ Seek help without entering identity information

An example of how this should be done would be:

Instead of

❌ “I go to St____ School where I was given a project…”

You would enter

✔ “I have a project I’m doing about…”

⭐ 7. Thinking that AI can do everything

Mistake:

Expecting that AI can: help you overcome feelings; Fix your issues at home, work, or school; Replace a teacher; Make a decision on your behalf.

Why it’s a problem:

Emotional and real-life related problems are not jobs for AI.

How to fix:

✔ Use AI only to assist with studies.

#8:Inevitably depending on AI will destroy your creativity.

Mistake:

You use AI for ideas, projects, or writing a sentence every time.

Why it's a problem:

Your creativity will fade and be less effective on your own.

How to prevent it:

  • ✔ Try brainstorming your own ideas first.
  • ✔ Use AI for guidance, not an alternate source.
  • ✔ Combine your ideas with the ideas AI generates.

#9: Not understanding what AI cannot do.

Mistake:

Many individuals will have developed an expectation that AI:

  • Will know all.
  • Has emotional intelligence.
  • Will understand the world the way we do.
  • Will read what I am thinking.

Why it's a problem:

These perceptions create false expectations about what AI can do.

How to prevent it:

  • ✔ Remember that AI will follow patterns, not emotions.
  • ✔ AI "thinks" differently than humans do.
  • ✔ You will ultimately be responsible for evaluating AI-generated results.

#10: Use of AI tools without verifying their safety.

Mistake:

Many teenagers are downloading "AI apps" from unknown sources (or through unknown websites).

Why it's a problem:

These risks include:

  • Data theft.
  • Malware.
  • Fake apps.
  • Scams.

How to prevent it:

  • ✔ Only use trusted websites and platforms.
  • ✔ Do not download "moded" or "cracked" apps.
  • ✔ Before using any tool, read through other users' reviews.

#11: Using AI for the purpose of harassing individuals or spreading false information.

Mistake:

Individuals are generating false screenshots of real conversations, false photos, or fake stories.

Why it's a problem:

This is unethical and has the potential to harm real people.

How to prevent it:

  • ✔ Use AI tools responsibly.
  • ✔ Never create false information that can cause harm to others.
  • ✔ Always respect others' privacy and get their permission.

#12: Expecting the same AI tool will work for every situation.

Mistake:

Individuals expect one AI app will take care of everything.

The Conclusion: Incorrect Use of AI Analysis; Business Beginners and Users Should Learn How To Use AI Effectively.

Simple rule for use of AI:

THE POWER OF AI DEPENDS ON YOUR USE OF AI!

The Effective And Proper Use Of AI Requires:

>>> PLAN AHEAD >>> CLEARLY ASK QUESTIONS >>> VERIFY INFORMATION >>> VIEW AI AS A TEACHER, AND NOT A CHEAT >>> MAINTAIN CONFIDENTIALITY >>> MAINTAIN SKILL LEVEL BY PRACTICING REAL WORLD SKILLS >>> ALWAYS BE ETHICAL AND RESPONSIBLE IN YOUR USE OF AI!
